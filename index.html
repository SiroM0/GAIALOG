View Prototype See Results

Project Overview

The Concept

Driven by the accumulation of electronic waste and the scarcity of Rare Earth Elements (REEs), GAIALOG repurposes discarded electronics into functional scientific instruments.

The Device

A Data Logger system built from recycled components (Arduino, DHT11, Ultrasonic sensors) capable of measuring temperature, humidity, and distance with high accuracy.

The Impact

Addressing pollution, reducing demand for mining, and providing cost-effective educational tools for Egyptian laboratories.

Egypt's Grand Challenge

Improving Scientific & Technological Environments

Scientific progress is the foundation of development. However, Egypt faces significant hurdles:

  • E-Waste Accumulation: WHO (2024) estimates massive e-waste growth, leading to soil and water pollution from neurotoxins like lead.
  • Resource Scarcity: High demand for Rare Earth Elements (REEs) vs. limited supply.
  • Economic Strain: High cost of importing scientific equipment limits educational access.
62M Tons of E-Waste (Global 2022)
22.3% Only Recycled

The Solution & Methodology

We built a Data Logger using components salvaged from discarded electronics.


01

Extraction

Salvaging ATmega328p from old Arduinos, DHT11 from broken weather kits, and LCDs from calculators.

02

Inspection

Testing components for safety and functionality before integration.

03

Construction

Assembling the circuit on a breadboard with capacitors and crystals to ensure stable voltage.

04

Calibration

Programming and testing against standard lab instruments to verify ±5% accuracy.

Recycled Components Used

ATmega328p DHT11 Module HC-SR04 Sensor 16x2 LCD Crystal Oscillator Resistors Capacitors
